One Page Redesign

Local Brewing Company

Project Type

High Fidelity Wireframe

Role

UI Designer

Overview

We were tasked to redesign a local business' website and condense the information to a single page. Links, apart from social media, could not lead to another pag, however, anchor tags to IDs on the page were allowed.

Content Audit

In the content auditing process I had to decide which information was needed or could still be relevant even after condensing in to a one page format. With a brewery it was obvious that the beer was what needed to focused on. So I began looking at accordian layouts and researching what type of layout I could achieve. Their brewery membership pricing and information was put onto a pdf so I needed to display the key points.
Another feature of the site was the ability to book their Taproom space, which could be condensed. While they had separate pages for corporate and private events, they were the exact same form.

UI Discovery

My focus was almost entirely on the beer selection. I had to make sure the information that they decided was important regarding their product was evident while also keeping to one page. The information they had (ABV, IBU, Beer Type etc.) were all necessary and couldn't neceessarily be left out.
Because of the extensive information on the page, there needed to be a way to navigate the entirety of the page easily. I chose to create a fixed navbar on the side of the page that would contain anchor tags that would take you the specific sections of the site on the page.

Visual Design

The website and its products, in this case the cans, had a disconnect in visual identity. So I began to draw from the industrial motifs of the cans and apply that to the website itself. Which proved to be reward because there was a cohesiveness to the UI elements now.

Accordian

My crown jewel of the project was indeed the accordian I chose to have tabs that would toggle between their seasonal and core batches. I designed icons that would denote whether it was available as a keg, in the taproom or in case. IBV and ABV are included at the bottom using their bar graphic but updated to the identity I implemented in this iteration.
